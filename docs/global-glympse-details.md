## Global Glimpse Program Notes

- Delegation size is typically 16-22 students with 4 adults: 2 Global Glimpse Leaders and 2 Program Coordinators.
- Phones are collected upon arrival in-country and stored for the duration of the program. This is a low-tech trip.
- Families should expect a safe-arrival call when students land or the following morning after a late arrival.
- Students usually get about two additional scheduled calls home, about 15 minutes each.
- Families can follow updates through Global Glimpse trip blogs at `globalglimpse.org/our-blogs/trip-blogs`.
- The program is educational/service-learning, not a vacation. Panama's country-specific focus in the slide deck is biocultural diversity.
- Community Action Project work is usually 2-3 days and may be part of a larger local partner project.
- Students may be "Leader of the Day" and should have a watch/alarm option that does not depend on their phone.

## Panama-Specific Requirements

- The Global Glimpse slide deck says US passports for Panama must have 3 months of extra validity.
- No vaccinations are required by Global Glimpse, but the slide deck recommends consulting CDC guidance and a doctor. It lists routine vaccines, Hepatitis A, Hepatitis B, and Typhoid as examples to discuss.
- Las Tablas/Chitre summer weather: generally warm and humid, about 75-86 F, with rain about 40% of summer days.
- Las Tablas delegations may do reforestation in mangrove forests, so a hat and boots or sturdy shoes are recommended.
- Electrical current is 110 AC/60 Hz and plugs are identical to US plugs.
