## Entry and Documents

- US citizens generally do not need a tourist visa for Panama for ordinary short tourist stays.
- The Embassy of Panama lists the United States among no-visa-required countries and notes that US and Canadian citizens may stay up to 180 days as tourists.
- Embassy guidance also mentions at least 3 months passport validity, a round-trip ticket, and proof of economic solvency.
- Bring printed or offline copies of:
  - Passport photo page.
  - Flight itinerary and onward or return ticket.
  - Global Glimpse trip details.
  - Health insurance details.
  - Travel insurance details. Global Glimpse says basic travel insurance is included through Cigna Global for qualified services during the program abroad.
  - Emergency contacts.
  - Prescriptions by generic name.
  - Green Card, visa, or I-512L if applicable.

## Health

- Check routine vaccines before travel.
- Global Glimpse says no vaccinations are required for its countries, but recommends consulting CDC guidance and a doctor. The slide deck lists routine vaccines, Hepatitis A, Hepatitis B, and Typhoid as examples to discuss.
- CDC recommends taking bug-bite prevention seriously in Panama: repellent, long sleeves/pants when appropriate, screened or air-conditioned rooms, and permethrin-treated gear for heavier outdoor trips.
- Malaria is a risk in some parts of Panama. If the route includes risk areas, talk to a clinician before leaving because some medicines need to start before departure.
- Dengue and other mosquito-borne illnesses are a practical concern, especially in warm/rainy conditions.
- Bring a small travel health kit:
  - Prescription meds in original containers when possible, with enough for at least two weeks.
  - Split prescription meds between carry-on and checked luggage when practical.
  - Copy of prescriptions or doctor's letter for specialized medications.
  - Two inhalers if Stephen uses an inhaler; EpiPen/allergy medicine if relevant.
  - Anti-diarrheal and electrolyte packets or oral rehydration salts.
  - Pain reliever/fever reducer.
  - Bandages/blister care.
  - Hydrocortisone or anti-itch treatment.
  - SPF 50+ sunscreen and after-sun care.
  - Mosquito repellent. Global Glimpse repeats CDC guidance favoring DEET and notes students may need it daily.

## Weather and Clothing

Panama is tropical. Global Glimpse's Panama slide lists Chitre and Las Tablas as about 75-86 F in summer, generally warm and humid, with rain about 40% of the days. Expect downpours and cold air conditioning.

Pack:

- 5-6 pants and 2-4 shorts that meet Global Glimpse dress code.
- 7-8 short-sleeve shirts and 3-4 lightweight long-sleeve shirts.
- 1 sweatshirt, fleece, windbreaker, or light jacket.
- Light rain shell or compact umbrella.
- Supportive closed-toe sneakers or running shoes with good traction. Open-toed shoes are not allowed outside accommodations.
- Flip-flops or sandals only for the accommodation.
- Sun hat and sunglasses.
- Sturdy swimsuit. Global Glimpse does not allow string bikinis or speedos.
- One clean travel-day outfit packed in carry-on.
- 1-2 sets of clothes that can get dirty for Community Action Project work.
- For Las Tablas only: Global Glimpse says CAP work may involve reforestation in mangrove forests, so bring a hat and boots or sturdy shoes.

## Money and Phones

- Panama uses the US dollar in practice, with Panamanian balboas as coins alongside US coins.
- Global Glimpse recommends $100-$200 in US currency and small bills. Budget about $15-$50 for airport/travel-day meals and $15-$20 for laundry.
- Newark Airport may not widely accept cash, so bring snacks, a card, or Apple/Google Pay for travel day.
- ATM/credit cards are allowed but not encouraged by Global Glimpse. Money kept in the room is the student's responsibility, though GG Leaders may be able to lock money up.
- Phones are collected upon arrival in-country. Students should write down important phone numbers they do not have memorized.
- Students may have two scheduled calls home, and families may receive calls from unknown or international numbers. WhatsApp is useful for international calls.
- Bring non-phone replacements as needed: camera without Wi-Fi, watch/alarm clock, flashlight, notebook, photos, and music device without Wi-Fi.

## Transport

- Global Glimpse says walking is often the main mode of transportation during the program, so shoes matter.
- Most group travel is by vetted private buses or vans with certified drivers. The group may use public buses once or twice with Program Coordinators.
- Host communities are described by Global Glimpse as safe and established, with students spending little time in capital cities.

## Safety

- Global Glimpse rules require students to stay accounted for and not leave the group without permission.
- The "big 5" rules: no tobacco products, no alcohol or illegal drugs, no sexual behavior or coupling, no new tattoos or piercings, and no threats of physical/emotional/sexual violence even as a joke.
- Night rules: 9:00 PM quiet time, 10:30 PM in assigned rooms/bathrooms preparing for lights out, 11:00 PM lights out. Leaders may move lights out earlier.
- Free time is short and controlled. Students may go out only in groups of 4 or more to approved locations, with sign-out/sign-in.
- Beach/water rules: no water past waist in open water without a life vest; no swimming alone; stay within leader-set boundaries.
- Failure to follow rules can lead to dismissal and being sent home at family expense, estimated by Global Glimpse at about $1,500.

## Program Experience

- This is an educational service-learning trip, not a vacation.
- Panama's country-specific focus in the Global Glimpse slide deck is biocultural diversity.
- The Community Action Project is usually 2-3 days of the trip and follows discover/design/deliver phases.
- Students may serve as Leader of the Day: waking peers, leading nightly meetings/energizers, writing the trip blog, maintaining morale, introducing speakers, and keeping the group hydrated.
- Accommodation is usually dorm-style with 1-4 pre-assigned roommates, basic showers, electricity, and possibly mosquito nets. Most places may not have air conditioning.
- Global Glimpse says safe drinking water is available at all times and is purified in Panama.
- Families can follow student trip blogs at `globalglimpse.org/our-blogs/trip-blogs`, but missed or delayed posts are normal because days are busy and internet may be inconsistent.
