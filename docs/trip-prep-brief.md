# Panama Trip Prep Brief

Last checked: 2026-07-14

This is a starter brief for Stephen's Panama trip. It is intentionally practical and should be updated once dates, regions, lodging, and activities are known.

## Fast Decisions

- Confirm passport expiration. Panama sources commonly require at least 3 months of passport validity; 6 months is the safer travel standard.
- Keep proof of onward or return travel handy.
- Carry a payment card and enough backup cash to satisfy proof-of-funds expectations if asked.
- Book a travel health appointment if the trip includes jungle, rural, island, or long outdoor time.
- Pack for heat, rain, sun, and mosquitoes.
- Decide whether the route includes malaria-risk areas before departure.

## Entry and Documents

- US citizens generally do not need a tourist visa for Panama for ordinary short tourist stays.
- The Embassy of Panama lists the United States among no-visa-required countries and notes that US and Canadian citizens may stay up to 180 days as tourists.
- Embassy guidance also mentions at least 3 months passport validity, a round-trip ticket, and proof of economic solvency.
- Bring printed or offline copies of:
  - Passport photo page.
  - Flight itinerary and onward or return ticket.
  - Lodging reservations.
  - Travel insurance details, if purchased.
  - Emergency contacts.
  - Prescriptions by generic name.

## Health

- Check routine vaccines before travel.
- CDC recommends taking bug-bite prevention seriously in Panama: repellent, long sleeves/pants when appropriate, screened or air-conditioned rooms, and permethrin-treated gear for heavier outdoor trips.
- Malaria is a risk in some parts of Panama. If the route includes risk areas, talk to a clinician before leaving because some medicines need to start before departure.
- Dengue and other mosquito-borne illnesses are a practical concern, especially in warm/rainy conditions.
- Bring a small travel health kit:
  - Prescription meds plus extra in case of delays.
  - Anti-diarrheal and oral rehydration salts.
  - Pain reliever/fever reducer.
  - Bandages/blister care.
  - Hydrocortisone or anti-itch treatment.
  - Sunscreen and after-sun care.
  - EPA-registered mosquito repellent.

## Weather and Clothing

Panama is tropical. Rain patterns vary by coast and elevation, but rainy-season travel usually means warm days, humidity, strong sun, and afternoon or sudden showers.

Pack:

- Lightweight breathable clothes.
- Light rain shell or compact umbrella.
- Quick-dry shoes or sandals that can handle wet ground.
- Sun hat and sunglasses.
- Swimsuit.
- One warmer layer if visiting Boquete, highlands, or heavily air-conditioned buses/hotels.
- Dry bag or zip bags for phone, passport copy, and electronics.

## Money and Phones

- Panama uses the US dollar in practice, with Panamanian balboas as coins alongside US coins.
- Carry small bills for taxis, tips, small shops, snacks, and areas where cards are not reliable.
- Notify banks only if the card issuer still recommends it.
- Consider an eSIM or roaming plan before departure. Keep offline maps downloaded.

## Transport

- Panama City: use official taxis, rideshare where available, or arranged transport. Avoid negotiating while tired at the airport if an arranged ride is easy.
- Intercity: domestic flights, shuttles, buses, and private drivers may all be reasonable depending on route.
- Driving: if renting a car, check whether an International Driving Permit is needed or useful, verify insurance, avoid night driving in unfamiliar rural areas, and expect wet-road conditions in rainy season.

## Safety

- Keep normal city awareness in Panama City: watch bags, avoid flashing valuables, and use known transport.
- Avoid the Darien Gap and border-adjacent remote areas unless traveling with a serious, reputable operator and current security guidance.
- For beaches and islands, pay attention to currents, boat safety, weather, and local advice.
- Share the itinerary and lodging addresses with a trusted person.
- Consider medical evacuation coverage if the trip includes remote regions, islands, hiking, or boating.

## Itinerary Building Blocks

These are common Panama trip anchors. Choose based on Stephen's dates and interests.

- Panama City: Casco Viejo, Panama Canal/Miraflores, Amador Causeway, food, museums.
- Canal/nearby nature: Soberania National Park, Pipeline Road, Gamboa, monkey/island tours.
- Highlands: Boquete, coffee tours, hiking, cooler weather.
- Caribbean islands: Bocas del Toro for beaches, boating, snorkeling, and rain-forest feel.
- San Blas/Guna Yala: beautiful but logistically specific; confirm transport, lodging simplicity, cash needs, and community rules.
- Pacific coast: beach towns and easier road access from Panama City.
